Goldbach Conjectures: Weak vs Strong

Weak: \(N_{\text{odd}} = p_1 + p_2 + p_3\). Strong: \(N_{\text{even}} = p_1 + p_2\).

Scroll to explore the story leading to the proof of the weak Goldbach Conjecture.

1. Winter 1954, Xiamen Air Raid Shelter

A cold coastal night. Sirens wail as 21‑year‑old Chen Jingrun reads by kerosene lamp, unfazed by chaos.

2. High School Lesson & the Teacher’s Proclamation

In a 1949 classroom the teacher declares Mathematics the queen of sciences and Goldbach its pearl. Classmates laugh—young Chen remains determined.

3. Christian Goldbach’s 1742 Journey & Letter

Goldbach proposes that every integer >2 is \(n = p_1 + p_2 + p_3\). Euler restates the even case \(2k = p_1 + p_2\).

4. Counting Representations with h(N)

Define \(h(N)\) as the number of unordered prime pairs \(p \le q\) with \(p+q=N\). Example: \(h(4)=1\), \(h(20)=2\).

5. Hilbert’s 1900 Challenge & Counting H(n)

At the 1900 ICM, Hilbert lists Goldbach among the 23 great problems, shifting focus to counting representations \(H(n)\).

6. Ramanujan’s Letter & the Birth of the Circle Method

In 1913 Ramanujan’s letter inspires Hardy and Littlewood, leading to the circle method of analytic number theory.

7. Vinogradov’s 1937 Breakthrough

Ivan Vinogradov proves that sufficiently large odd numbers are the sum of three primes, shrinking the bound over time.

8. Chen Jingrun’s “1+2” Theorem & Cultural Revolution

In 1966 Chen showed that every large even \(n\) satisfies \(n = p + (q \text{ or } q_1 q_2)\), all while facing hardship.

9. Helfgott’s 2013 Proof of the Weak Conjecture

Harald Helfgott refines the circle method and computer‑verifies cases up to 8.8×10³⁰, proving the ternary Goldbach conjecture.

10. Modern Computational Verifications & Goldbach’s Comet

From Nils Pipping’s 100k check to modern CPU clusters verifying up to 4×10¹⁸, the plot of H(n) versus n resembles a comet.

11. Conclusion & the Ongoing Quest

The weak conjecture is proven, but the strong conjecture remains open. Will you find the missing pearl?
